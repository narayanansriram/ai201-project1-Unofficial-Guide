[December 2022] Don't be afraid of this class. It's not a walk in the park, but it's also not rocket science. Many have mentioned that there's a big info-dump at the beginning, and they're right that you shouldn't be intimidated by that. It is reinforced by the later content, and it's actually a good idea to go back and re-read those modules periodically through the course to clarify things. In this class, you're writing very basic programs, you just have less help from your programming language. You'll be moving bytes data around between the computer's memory and the processor's local memory, then doing very basic things like adding/subtracting/multiplying/dividing, converting things between binary/hex/text, sorting numbers and averaging them, and printing text on a screen. Nothing too mind-bending to understand if you give yourself time to learn. That said, you really do need time. It can take a long time to figure out how to do things with such primitive operations, it may take a little while for certain concepts to fully click, and it is very easy to lose track of the bytes you're moving around and end up wasting time (hours) debugging. Most of the projects can easily take over 10 hours to work on, and you also need time to do open-everything module exercises, two open-notes, open-internet quizzes, and two proctored exams where you only have access to a note-sheet and a binary/hex calculator. The exams are the most treacherous part because your time is tight and you can't rely on a debugger to make sure you aren't making little errors that add up over time. The exams are much more about running through simple programs on paper and reporting what things evaluate to at certain points in execution, but there are some conceptual questions and you should have a pretty good understanding of that content as well to get an A. YOU CAN GET AN A IN THIS CLASS - start early so you can do the extra credit and get help when you need it. You can fail both the midterm and final and still pass this class. All in all - a bit of a challenge and pretty demanding of your time, but I think it was a great learning experience and really gave me a peak behind the curtain of what higher level programming languages and the computer itself are actually doing when I write programs in a highly expressive language like Python.
